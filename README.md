## Overview
This project analyzes performance and workforce data across **4,000 employees** to uncover how department, work location, salary, and tenure relate to employee performance. The result is an interactive Power BI dashboard that helps HR teams identify trends and make data-informed decisions on promotions, retention, and workforce planning.

## Problem Statement
HR teams often lack a clear, unified view of how factors like department, salary, and work location relate to employee performance, making it difficult to make informed decisions on promotions, compensation, and retention.

## Objective
To analyze employee performance data and identify patterns across departments, work locations, salary bands, and tenure — helping HR teams make informed decisions on promotions, compensation, and workforce planning.

## Tools & Technologies
- **Excel** — Data cleaning (removed duplicates, handled blanks, standardized data types)
- **Power BI** — Data modeling and interactive dashboard design

## Data Source
Real employee data covering 4,000 employees, including department, work location, salary, tenure, performance category, and absence records.

## Process & Approach
1. Cleaned raw employee data in **Excel** — removed duplicate records, handled blank/missing values, standardized data types for consistency
2. Built an interactive **Power BI** dashboard with slicers (Gender, Department) for dynamic filtering
3. Designed visualizations to explore performance by work location, department, salary, and tenure
4. Segmented analysis to identify performance and compensation trends across the workforce

## Key Insights
- **4,000 total employees**, with an average performance score of **3.01**, average salary of **5.97K**, and a promotion rate of **1.00**
- Average absence days across the workforce is **7.52 days**
- **Employees by performance category:** the largest share fall into "Good" performance **(38.9%)**, followed by "Average" **(20.71%)**, with "Weak" performers at **19.85%**
- **Marketing** and **Engineering** departments have the highest average salaries, while **HR** trails behind
- Performance is fairly consistent across **Remote, In-office, and Hybrid** work locations, suggesting work location has minimal impact on performance
- **Sales and Engineering** departments show the strongest average performance scores, while HR shows relatively lower performance
- Performance fluctuates over employee tenure with no strong long-term trend, though slight dips appear at certain tenure milestones

## Recommendations
- Review HR department compensation structure, since it lags behind other departments despite comparable performance expectations
- Since work location shows minimal impact on performance, consider expanding remote/hybrid flexibility without added performance risk
- Investigate performance dips at specific tenure milestones to identify potential engagement or retention issues, and design targeted interventions (mentorship, career development check-ins) around those points
